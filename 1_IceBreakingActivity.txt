Hi everyone, let me share with you an overview about what I'll be working

My doctoral research focuses on developing a resilient monitoring architecture for Smart Grids. The goal is to design a MIMO (Multiple-Input Multiple-Output) optical sensor and communication system using Space Division Multiplexing (SDM) over optical fiber.



Essentially, we aim to transmit multiple critical electrical variables (voltages, currents from different systems) simultaneously through a single multimode fiber, ensuring data integrity in high-voltage environments where traditional electronic sensors fail due to electromagnetic interference. Currently, the project is in the process of defining the signal processing and recovery architecture.



Although my thesis advisor's main focus is optoelectronics and signal processing, I guess, it could be possible to use computer vision to provide innovative solutions for information processing at the "edge," for Power Quality Classification Using Images: which I propose to explore as part of the experiments for this course, so it could be relevant in my research.



I guess, the use of a "edge" device could be an extra, but it depends on availability and future talks with my supervisor, then only softaware is always an alternative.
